11.1 Conservation equations

\[ \begin{pmatrix} \text{rate of change}\\ \text{ in the population density} \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} \text{spatial movement} \end{pmatrix} + \begin{pmatrix} \text{birth, growth, death},\\ \text{production or degradation}\\ \text{due to chemical reactions} \end{pmatrix} \]

11.2 Deriving spatially homogeneous models

\[ \begin{aligned} N(t+\Delta t) &= \quad N(t) + \quad K N(t) \Delta t. \end{aligned} \tag{11.1}\]

11.3 A model for cell growth under nutrient depletion

\[ \begin{aligned} \frac{dN}{dt} &= K(c) N = \kappa cN, \\ \frac{ dc}{dt} &= - \alpha \frac{dN}{dt} = - \alpha \kappa c N, \end{aligned} \tag{11.2}\]

11.4 Logistic growth equation

The last equation can be rewritten as \[ \frac{dN}{dt} = \rho N \, (1 - \frac N B) \qquad \quad N(0)= N_0, \tag{11.3}\]

11.5 Exercise

Consider a well mixed bio reactor.

A biologist cultures an initial cell population of size \(N_0\) in the bioreactor for 72 h.

Cells undergo division with a period of 14 h.

Each cell produces a non-degradable waste product, \(W\), at rate \(k_1\).

When total waste levels exceed a threshold, \(W^*\), cell division stops. Otherwise the cell population grows exponentially.

How many cells are there at the end of the experiment?

12.1 The SIR model

Consider the SIR model equations: \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{d S}{ dt} &= -rIS, \\ \frac{d I}{ dt} &= rIS - aI, \\ \frac{d R}{ dt} &= aI. \end{aligned} \]

What are the variables? What are the parameters?

Identify an expression for the reproduction number, \(R_0\).

Hence explain why the condition \(R_0<1\) is necessary to avoid an epidemic?

12.2 An activator inhibitor model

Consider the reqction schematic \[ 2A+B \rightarrow A. \]

Assume that species A is produced at constant rate \(k_1\) and degrades at rate \(k_2\).

Assume that A is linearly degraded.

Hence obtain the ODEs \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{d A}{ dt} &= k_1 - k_2 A + k_3 A^2 B, \\ \frac{d B}{ dt} &= k_4 - k_3 A^2 B, \end{aligned} \]

Identify the steady state of the ODEs.
